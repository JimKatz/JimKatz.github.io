Step 2 - Song Setup

  1. 4. After you've downloaded and Installed OpenMPT, Open the program and drag the midi file you want to use into the top left area.

    5. After you do that, click the "General" tab and look for a property box labeled "MPTM (OpenMPT)"

Step 5 - Lowering Sample Size


  • Unfortunately, you will probably need to do this a lot, songs can only be a certain size in the game and samples usually take up a ton of space, so you have to lower the sample size

    There are 2 ways to do this.

    Method #1 : ​Downsampling
     
  •  Go into the instruments folder of your song. It will look like this (but with your instruments)

  •  
  • One at a time, click on every instrument, This will bring up the instrument page for that instrument.
    When the page comes up, click on the "Sample" Tab, then press CTRL+R, it should bring up this window.


     

  • Make sure Downsample is selected then hit OK.

    Do this for all of your instruments.

    Hopefully, you will only need to do this once, but sometimes, you may need to do this twice or even 3 times.

    If you chose this method and downsampled all of your instruments, move on to Step 6.



  • Method #2 : ​Pitch Shift
     
  •  Go into the instruments folder of your song. It will look like this (but with your instruments)

  •  
  • Click on an instrument, This will bring up the instrument page for that instrument.
    When the page comes up, click on the "Sample" Tab.

    On the sample page, look for the "Transpose" setting and click on it.


  •  
  • Set the pitch to a high amount. But make sure to count how many entries
    are between the original pitch and the new pitch you select.

    When you've counted the number of entries, go into the
  • Patterns tab and find the channel that plays the instrument this sample is linked to.

    Click on the top channel box and select "Transpose Channel"



    When it asks for the amount to transpose by, you will enter the number of entries
    between the original pitch and your new one (as a negative number).

    Example : 
    Original Pitch : B7
    New Pitch : E8

    For this, I would tranpose by -5


    Unfortunately, there isn't a setting to transpose an entire channel in OpenMPT,
    so you will have to do this for every pattern the instrument is used in...

  • If you chose this method and pitch shifted all of your samples, move on to Step 6.
